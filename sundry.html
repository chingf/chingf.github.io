RECENT READS/WRITINGS

A few particularly enjoyable works:
  • If On a Winter's Night a Traveller by Italo Calvino
    • An intriguing work of recursive literature, calling into question the role of the reader in the novel.
  • Extremely Loud and Incredibly Close by Jonathan Safran Foer
    • A beautiful and emotional story about how a young boy copes with tragedy post-9/11.
  • Zeitoun by Dave Eggers
    • Sort of a revamp of What is the What, but nonetheless a well written novel concluding with human strength and resilience.
  • The Savage Detectives by Roberto Bolaño
    • An expansive novel about Mexico City poets, by one of the greatest Latino voices in literature.
    Dazzling and unique, the novel is told from over fifty characters.
  • Goodnight, Punpun by Inio Asano
    • A manga combining gorgeous art with the increasingly sorrowful narrative of Punpun Onodera's life.
    Asano makes the unique choice of representing the character as a crudely drawn bird,
    with the shape morphing as his mental psyche becomes more unstable.
